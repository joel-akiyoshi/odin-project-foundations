Miso Soup

Description

Miso soup is a classic Japanese soup made with miso paste and dashi broth. It’s light, comforting, and pairs beautifully with rice or noodles. You can add tofu, green onions, or seaweed for extra flavor and texture.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups water
  • 2 tsp dashi powder (or 4 cups prepared dashi broth)
  • 3 tbsp miso paste (white or red miso)
  • 1/2 cup cubed tofu (silken or firm)
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • 1 sheet dried seaweed (wakame), rehydrated and chopped

Instructions

  1. In a medium pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil. Add dashi powder and stir until dissolved.
  2. Lower the heat to a gentle simmer. Add tofu cubes and wakame seaweed. Cook for 2–3 minutes.
  3. In a small bowl, scoop out some of the hot broth and whisk in the miso paste until smooth. This prevents clumping.
  4. Pour the miso mixture back into the pot. Do not boil after adding miso—this preserves its flavor and probiotics.
  5. Turn off the heat and stir in the sliced green onions.
  6. Serve warm as a side dish or enjoy it on its own with rice or udon noodles.

Tips

  • Try mixing white and red miso for a deeper flavor.
  • Add mushrooms, clams, or shrimp for a heartier version.
  • If you don’t have dashi powder, you can use vegetable broth and a small piece of kombu (sea kelp).
